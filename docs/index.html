On the Open Web and Its Enemies

DOCUMENT REF: DPN-001  |  CLASSIFICATION: PUBLIC  |  STATUS: ACTIVE

The open web is under siege. Not by armies or governments alone, but by something more insidious — a quiet, systematic architecture of surveillance woven into every page you visit, every search you make, every link you click. You are not a user. You are a data point. A behavioral profile. A commodity traded in millisecond auctions before your page even loads.

Every cookie dropped on your machine is a listening device. Every "free" service is a contract you never read and never signed. Every personalized ad is proof that someone, somewhere, has built a model of who you are, what you want, and how to manipulate you into wanting more.

"The most effective prison is the one where the inmates don't know they're imprisoned."

We are Destopian Pirate — a decentralized collective of developers, privacy advocates, and digital dissidents who refuse to accept this as normal. We don't write petitions. We don't file complaints. We write code.

Our tools are weapons of digital self-defense. They don't phone home. They don't harvest data. They don't serve ads. They don't compromise. They exist for one purpose: to give you back what was systematically taken from you — your privacy, your autonomy, your right to browse the web without being watched, tracked, profiled, and sold.

We build in the open because transparency is not a feature — it's a requirement. Every line of code is public. Every decision is auditable. We don't ask for trust. We provide proof.

The watchers have no power here.

Intelligence Reports

Detailed analysis from resistance operatives.

REPORT DPN-IR-001 HIGH

Manifest V3 Transition Analysis

Chrome's shift to Manifest V3 fundamentally changes how ad blockers operate. The move from webRequest (blocking) to declarativeNetRequest limits dynamic rule evaluation. Our analysis: the constraint is real but manageable. Destopian AdBlock Pro was built MV3-native from the ground up — no migration required, no capability loss.

2026.06.10 CLASSIFICATION: PUBLIC
REPORT DPN-IR-002 MEDIUM

CNAME Cloaking Threat Vector

Advertisers are increasingly using CNAME records to disguise tracker domains as first-party subdomains, bypassing traditional domain-based blocking. This technique is proliferating among major ad networks. We are evaluating countermeasures for future tool versions.

2026.06.09 CLASSIFICATION: PUBLIC
REPORT DPN-IR-003 INFO

Browser Fingerprinting Report

Even without cookies, modern tracking techniques can identify users via browser fingerprinting — screen resolution, installed fonts, WebGL renderer, canvas hashing, and more. Current generation tools focus on request blocking; fingerprint resistance is on our roadmap.

2026.06.08 CLASSIFICATION: PUBLIC
REPORT DPN-IR-004 INFO

First-Party Data Collection Trends

As third-party cookies phase out, major platforms are pivoting to first-party data strategies — walled gardens where they control both the content and the tracking infrastructure. This makes browser-level protection increasingly critical.

About the Network

Who we are, how we started, and where we're going.

The Origin

Destopian Pirate was born from a simple frustration: the modern web has become hostile to the people who use it. Every page loaded is a negotiation between you and dozens of invisible entities fighting for your attention, your data, and your trust.

We started building because we realized that complaints don't change systems. Code does. The first project — Destopian AdBlock Pro — wasn't designed to compete with existing ad blockers. It was designed to prove that you can build effective, privacy-respecting tools without any compromises.

No "acceptable ads" programs. No whitelist deals with ad networks. No analytics SDK bundled into the extension. Just clean code that does exactly what it claims and nothing else.

The Mission

We're building a toolkit for digital self-defense. Each extension addresses a specific threat vector in the surveillance ecosystem — from ad blocking to tracker prevention to fingerprint resistance. Every tool is built Manifest V3 native, open source, and privacy-first from day one.

Technical Infrastructure

Technology Stack

The tools and standards behind the resistance.

Manifest V3

Built on Chrome's latest extension platform from the ground up. No legacy migration, no compatibility hacks. Native declarative architecture.

Declarative Net Request

Blocking rules execute in the browser's network stack itself — faster than JavaScript-based filtering and more resistant to anti-adblock circumvention.

Service Workers

Event-driven background processing. No persistent background page consuming memory. Activates only when needed, sleeps when idle.
